# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Repsync serves four connected user groups:

- Independent personal trainers who need one place to run their coaching business, manage leads, publish packages, deliver plans, and follow client progress.
- Online coaches with multiple clients who need fast scanning, repeatable workflows, and clear visibility across onboarding, check-ins, messages, nutrition, training, billing, and risk.
- PT teams who coordinate client delivery together and need shared operational context without losing accountability or creating dashboard clutter.
- Clients who discover trainers through public profile and marketplace surfaces, then use Repsync independently to create, track, and improve their fitness and nutrition.

The default context is an authenticated product workspace. Users are often trying to act quickly, compare status across several people or spaces, and understand what needs attention next.

## Product Purpose

Repsync is a web-first SaaS product for personal training businesses and their clients. It connects public trainer discovery, lead intake, coaching operations, client management, training delivery, nutrition tracking, and progress visibility into one coherent system.

Success means trainers and teams can run the business without juggling disconnected tools, clients can understand and track their own progress, and public marketplace/profile surfaces can create trust before a coaching relationship starts.

The product should prioritize clarity, momentum, and decision support. The interface should help users know where they are, what matters now, and what action to take next.

## Brand Personality

Premium, easy to use, and insightful.

Repsync should feel capable and polished without becoming intimidating. It should have the confidence of a serious operating system for coaching, with enough warmth and clarity that clients and solo trainers can use it without training.

## Anti-references

Repsync should not feel like:

- A generic AI SaaS dashboard with interchangeable cards, vague gradients, and ornamental metrics.
- A childish fitness app with cartoonish energy, gamified clutter, or weak professional credibility.
- A sterile medical portal that feels cold, bureaucratic, or disconnected from coaching momentum.
- A crypto or neon dashboard with excessive glow, dark visual noise, and speculative-finance aesthetics.
- A Notion clone with overly flat hierarchy, generic blocks, and low visual ownership.

It should also avoid marketing-page patterns inside operational workspaces, including oversized hero sections, decorative copy, and repeated shortcut grids that delay real action.

## Design Principles

1. Lead with the next useful action.
   Every operational screen should make the most important status and next step obvious before decorative or secondary content appears.

2. Make complex coaching work feel calm.
   Dense information is acceptable, but hierarchy, spacing, contrast, and copy must keep the product easy to scan under load.

3. Earn premium through restraint.
   Use rich surfaces, motion, and atmosphere to support trust and focus, not to show off. The interface should feel expensive because it is coherent and precise.

4. Serve both business and client confidence.
   Trainer-facing workflows should feel decisive and efficient. Client and marketplace surfaces should feel clear, encouraging, and trustworthy.

5. Keep the system consistent before inventing.
   Reuse established shells, tokens, typography, components, and interaction patterns unless a new pattern solves a real workflow problem.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Repsync should target WCAG AA by default.

Accessibility expectations:

- Reduced-motion support for animated backgrounds, transitions, and interactive effects.
- Color-blind safe state communication, with icons, labels, badges, or text in addition to color.
- Strong contrast in both dark and light themes, including muted text, dropdowns, inputs, disabled states, and focus rings.
- Keyboard-accessible navigation, menus, dialogs, filters, and primary workflows.
- Clear labels, helper text where it prevents confusion, and error messages placed near the relevant control.
- Responsive layouts that remain usable at mobile, tablet, desktop, and wide desktop sizes.
